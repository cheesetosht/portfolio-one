Yesterday, I spent more than 12 hours just trying to fix my Arch linux enviroment. Reinstalled it & set everything up from scratch 4 times. Everytime something or the other kept breaking.

After a lot of research & trying different configuration, I finally decided to settle with Manjaro with GNOME on top, still Arch but I read that it comes with a lot of sane defaults & a GUI. So even if something breaks & I have to reinstall it, I won't have to type 10 commands just to setup the WiFi driver.

I had been using Arch + KDE as my daily driver for about 6 months. It all started with my frustration windows taking half of my RAM in idle, aspiration of having a Mac-like experience and using a real UNIX system (not WSL). Even after trying Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xbuntu, Linux Mint & Deepin, my persuit for the ultimate optimisation & customisation didn't stop. I finally sat with the 2 hour installation video & my notebook. Back then I didn't understand any of the command, I just tried to follow the order. After 2 days of stuggle I had my first stable arch setup. Something else also happened in these 2 day, I stumbled across the _rich_ community of Arch users. Then every few weeks tried new configurations, tools & customisations. At some point I also got it to look & work almost like MacOS.

![arch linux with macos vibes](/writings/arch-setup.webp)

Then I got busy & everything was smooth sailing. Unless I wanted to use adobe tools or play games, I never booted windows. I was a proud arch user with no regret until one software update. I started facing bugs & glitches, the perfection I worked so hard for started vanishing.

It was time I start looking for another distro. I also started realizing the cons of desktop environments like KDE & XFCE. My priorities shifted from customisation & control to reliability. I couldn't afford to spend time reinstalling my audio drivers because they stopped working before a meeting.

I never liked any of the debian-based distros. They are supposed to be stable but that also means that if something is broken, it stays broken for longer. With arch & it's community, everything is bleeding-edge. That's the reason why I ended up with Manjaro + GNOME, bleeding-edge but lesser odds of bleeding myself to death.

However, this was my journey. Let's see how it goes. This was one of those rabbit holes that I'm glad I went into, so many learnings. I can only imagine how nightmarish it would've been if this was a server instead of a PC.
