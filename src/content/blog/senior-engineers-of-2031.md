There was a time when companies used to hire a large group of early engineers & spent a good chunks of budget on training them. You might think that due to the onset of agentic tools, the "Senior Engineer + Agent" combo made junior engineers obsolete. Even I used to think so until I dived deeper. I realised that even when I started coding in 2019, there were very few companies that were willing to spend on training juniors, ChatGPT came into the picture in late 2022. Training was mostly outsourced to youtube channels & bootcamps, we'll come to the reason for this a bit later because it has a learning for us.

However, it's important for us to first see the situation we are in right now first.

1. Execs: The Prophets of AI acceleration
2. Economics (interest rates)

The situation can be imagined as your dad taking over the wheel in a bad traffic. This looks like a safe & wise decision but the outcome of this decision is a bit tricky. He would've saved the day (or probably just the car) once pushing you to level up a bit.

The agentic engineer burnout is also a concerning factor, an average engineer's life sounds much more fun when they are brainstorming with the team, exchanging knowledge & mentoring juniors compared to just reviewing 10x more code that looks correct but can be hallucinated
