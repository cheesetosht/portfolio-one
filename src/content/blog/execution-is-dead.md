speed has always been THE MOAT. all we've heard from the founders & PMs is MVP, TAM, TAT & what not. the last whole decade or even more than that was all about moving fast, doing more, more features, more products, entire suite of products, multiple suites of products.

that's what everyone was optimising for, be it companies, products or individuals. but with the AI shift coming in, execution is almost solved.

so what matters now? the answer is simple regardless of whoever you are. at this point I've read hundreds of blogs & articles making all sorts of claims about the capability of AI & the AI culture shift in general. i ended up noticing an interesting pattern. I can this is mostly true atleast for the tech industry. the cheatsheet for all the roles remains constant, just do what your superior suggested parking or deprioritising in the interest of speed

speaking of products, the baseline for what an MVP means has not only shifted higher, in fact building much better mvps has been commoditised. no wonder we see solo builders posting new MVPs everyday. so clearly MVPs are not difficult, we gotta push farther. and a very good starting point for this is looking at what we where asked not to do or couldn't afford to do.

visual designer: that's creating motion, 3d & video content
product designers: that's writing code
software engineers: that can be using something other than js or python
